WED Scoring
eSports for Engineers 24041LTS

Note: save your .pgn files after each session.  Copy your .pgn files,
along with screenshot .png files showing your games and analysis, to
the WED/afterGameReport directory, calculate your score as described
below, and then enter your score using the launcher.

Note: these OKRs can be completed with any combination of the chess
programs available on WED (nibbler, scid, banksiaGui, Chessmaster,
openingRepertoire).

OBJECTIVE #1: LEARN CHESS WELL ENOUGH TO PLAY IN A TOURNAMENT
OBJECTIVE #2: (ASPIRATIONAL) ACHIEVE A COMPETITIVE RATING
OBJECTIVE #3: USER DEFINED (can be regular or aspirational)
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 1, first key result for objective "learn chess"

Play a game against the AI at your current skill level.  Use
nibbler or banksiaGui to analyze the game afterward with a neural
network engine.

score=.1 if you played a complete game and analyzed it with an
engine, identifying at least 2 mistakes or missed tactics.
score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 2

Study one chess concept from WED/DOC/chessConceptsBySeriwan.md
or from the recommended videos in WED/DOC/recommendedVideos.txt.
Play 2 games where you apply that concept.

score=.1 if completed.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 3

Use openingRepertoire to study one opening line.  Practice the
line until you can play the first 10 moves from memory.

score=.1 if completed.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 4

Play a complete game against Chessmaster training mode (if installed)
or against AI at a level where you win about 50% of the time.

If you win: score=.1
If you draw: score=.05
If you lose but can identify your critical mistake: score=.025
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 5

The following tasks are worth .05 points each:

Skim all documentation in the WED/DOC directory

Watch an instructional chess video from WED/DOC/recommendedVideos.txt

Solve 10 tactical puzzles (available online or in scid)

Use scid to build a database of your games and review patterns

Study one endgame position (K+P vs K, K+R vs K, etc.) until you
can win it reliably
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #2, 1

Play a rated game (online or against strong AI) and achieve a
performance rating above your current rating.

If your performance exceeds your current rating:
score=1 good for the next month.

If not:
score=0.7*(performance/target_rating), with maximum score of .7

Note: Your daily total is the sum of your OKR scores (subject to an
upper limit of 0.7 unless you have an aspirational OKR score above 0.7).
