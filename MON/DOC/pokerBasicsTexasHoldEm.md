Poker Basics
------------

**Game Version**: No Limit Texas Hold'em **Objective**: Make the best 5-card poker hand using 2 personal cards and 5 shared cards, or successfully bluff

Hand Rankings (from lowest to highest)
--------------------------------------

1.  High Card
2.  One Pair
3.  Two Pair
4.  Three of a Kind
5.  Straight
6.  Flush
7.  Full House (Boat)
8.  Four of a Kind (Quads)
9.  Straight Flush
10.  Royal Flush

**Important Notes**:

*   Aces can be high or low in straights
*   No wrap-arounds allowed (e.g., Queen-King-Ace-2-3 is not a straight)
*   Royal Flush odds: approximately 30,000 to 1

Table Positions
---------------

1.  Button (Dealer)
2.  Small Blind (left of Button)
3.  Big Blind (left of Small Blind)

Betting Rounds
--------------

1.  Pre-Flop
2.  Flop
3.  Turn
4.  River

Player Actions
--------------

*   Check: Stay in without betting
*   Bet: Put chips into the pot
*   Call: Match the current bet
*   Raise: Increase the size of the bet
*   Fold: Throw away hand and surrender pot

Hand Structure
--------------

1.  Forced bets: Small and Big Blinds
2.  Deal 2 hole cards to each player
3.  Pre-flop betting round
4.  Deal 3 community cards (Flop)
5.  Flop betting round
6.  Deal 1 community card (Turn)
7.  Turn betting round
8.  Deal 1 community card (River)
9.  River betting round
10.  Showdown (if necessary)

Key Concepts
------------

*   Community cards: Shared cards in the middle of the table
*   Hole cards: Personal cards dealt to each player
*   Playing the board: Using all 5 community cards as your hand
*   Showdown: Revealing hands to determine the winner
*   Button rotation: Moves clockwise after each hand

Betting Rules
-------------

*   Minimum raise: Double the big blind
*   Maximum raise in No Limit: All-in (entire chip stack)
*   Action starts left of the big blind pre-flop
*   Action starts left of the button post-flop

Miscellaneous
-------------

*   Downloadable hand rankings guide available for reference
*   Online and casino games have dealers; home games may require player dealing
*   Practice and further learning recommended for improvement
