Hand Rankings
-------------

**Basic Hand Rankings (from worst to best):**

1.  High card
2.  Pair
3.  Two pair
4.  Three of a kind
5.  Straight
6.  Flush
7.  Full house
8.  Four of a kind
9.  Straight flush
10.  Royal flush

Specific Hand Types and Terminology
-----------------------------------

**Pair**: Two cards of the same value **Two Pair**: Two different pairs **Three of a Kind**:

*   Trips: Made up of two community cards and one hole card
*   Set: Made when a player has a pocket pair and hits their third card on the flop, turn, or river

**Straight**: Five cards in a row of different suits **Flush**: Five random cards of the same suit **Full House (Boat)**: Three of a kind plus a pair **Four of a Kind (Quads)**: Four cards of the same value **Straight Flush**: Five cards in a row all of the same suit **Royal Flush**: The best possible poker hand; a straight flush, ten to the ace

Additional Hand-Related Terms
-----------------------------

**Kicker**: A side card held alongside a pair, two pair, three of a kind, or four of a kind **Split Pot (Chop)**: When players have the exact same five-card hand, and the chips are split evenly **The Nuts**: The best possible hand **Second Nut**: The second-best possible hand **Nut Flush**: The best possible flush

Hole Cards and Starting Hands
-----------------------------

**Suited Hole Cards**: Two starting cards of the same suit **Off-suit Hole Cards**: Two starting cards not of the same suit **Connectors**: Two cards in consecutive order (e.g., Queen-Jack or 6-5) **Gappers**: Cards that are not quite connecting

*   One gapper: One space between cards (e.g., 10-8)
*   Two gappers, three gappers, etc.

Hand Strength and Potential
---------------------------

**Speculative Hand**: A weak hand with potential to improve with the right board **Marginal Hand**: A mediocre hand that could possibly win at showdown **Made Hand**: A complete hand with little potential to improve further **Monster**: An extremely strong hand **Unmade**: A hand that needs to improve to win **Trash**: A really poor hand **Live**: A hand that could still win

Board Interactions
------------------

**Top Pair**: When a player connects one of their hole cards with the highest card on the board **Over Pair**: A pocket pair higher than all the cards on the board **Under Pair**: A pocket pair lower than the cards on the board **Dominated**: When a player shares a card with their opponent, but their other card is worse **Reverse Domination**: When a dominated hand catches and takes the lead or wins

Range and Board Textures
------------------------

**Range**: An array of hands a player might have based on community cards and betting patterns

*   Wide range: Many possibilities
*   Narrow range: Few possibilities

**Scare Card**: A card that significantly increases a player's chances of losing **Wet Board**: A situation where straights, flushes, or full houses can easily be made **Dry Board**: Few draws available, hard for players to have strong hands **Rainbow Board**: Community cards of different suits with no immediate flush draw **Monotone Board**: All community cards of the same suit

Draws and Outs
--------------

**Draw**: When a player needs specific card(s) to complete their hand **Open-ended Straight Draw**: Four cards in sequential order, needing one card higher or lower to complete **Gut Shot (Belly Buster)**: Needing a single value card in the middle of a straight draw **Double Belly Buster (Double Gutter)**: Two different cards in the middle that would complete the straight **Runner Runner**: Both turn and river provide cards to complete a player's draw **Backdoor Draw**: Needing runner runner to complete a draw **Outs**: Cards a player needs to hit to have the winning hand

Odds and Probabilities
----------------------

**Odds**: Chances of making a winning hand **Equity**: Odds expressed as a percentage **Coin Flip (Flipping/Racing)**: When two players have roughly equal chances of winning

Game Situations
---------------

**Heads Up**: Only two players competing for a pot or in the final stage of a tournament **Multi-way**: More than two players competing for a pot after the flop **Family Pot**: A multi-way pot involving every player at the table **Set Mining**: Getting involved with a small pair intending to make a set
