Study Guide: Betting and Raising in Texas Hold'em Poker

Key Concepts
------------

*   Betting: Putting chips into the pot
*   Three ways to put chips in the pot:
    
    1.  Making the initial bet
    2.  Calling a bet
    3.  Raising a bet
    
*   Two primary reasons to bet:
    
    1.  For value (when you think you have the best hand)
    2.  As a bluff (when you don't have the best hand)
    

Pre-Flop Betting
----------------

*   Players in the small and big blind have already posted the first bets
*   Raising is the only option to enter the pot pre-flop
*   Standard raise size: 2-3 big blinds, regardless of hole cards
*   Three-betting: Re-raising before the flop (the third bet)
*   Four-betting and five-betting: Re-raising and re-re-raising

Post-Flop Betting
-----------------

*   Continuation bet (c-bet): Bet made by the pre-flop aggressor on the flop
    
    *   C-betting for value: When you connect with the flop or think you have the best hand
    *   C-betting as a bluff: When you haven't connected with the board but want to pretend you have
    *   Best times to c-bet bluff: When there are no obvious draws and few players in the pot
    
*   Donk betting: Betting first after the flop as the player who called the original raise
    
    *   Can be effective in denying free cards and making opponents pay to continue
    
*   Check-raise: Checking when the action is on you, then raising after a player behind you bets

Bet Sizing
----------

*   Dry board (few draws possible): 25-30% of the pot
*   Wet board with possible straight draw: 50% of the pot or slightly more
*   Very wet board with straights and flushes possible: 75-80% of the pot
*   Against weak players with no understanding of odds: 90-100% of the pot for more value

Remember to be selective when bluffing, and consider factors like position, community cards, and other players at the table when sizing your bets.
