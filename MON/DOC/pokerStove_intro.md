Study Guide: Using PokerStove to Evaluate Poker Hands

1.  PokerStove Overview
    
    *   PokerStove is an approved program at all online poker sites and is not considered cheating [1]
    *   It allows you to evaluate poker hands and make decisions about calling or folding [1]
    
2.  Inputting Hands and Ranges in PokerStove
    
    *   Enter your own hand by typing the cards or selecting them from the cards menu [1]
    *   For opponent's hand, select a range of cards based on their actions and position [1]
        
        *   Use the pre-flop tab to select a percentage range of hands they could have [1]
        *   Alternatively, manually select specific hands from the card section based on the situation [1]
        
    
3.  Evaluating Hands on the Flop
    
    *   Input the flop cards in the board cards section [1]
    *   Click "Evaluate" to see the percentage of time you are ahead on the flop [1]
    *   Example: With pocket kings against opponent's top 20% range on an ace-high flop, you are about 60% to win [1]
    
4.  Deciding to Call or Fold Pre-Flop All-Ins
    
    *   Consider opponent's stack size and likely range of hands [1]
    *   Example: With pocket 8s against a 10-15 big blind all-in, calling is +EV if opponent's range includes enough weaker hands [1]
    *   Adding more pocket pairs to their range decreases your equity, potentially making it a fold [1]
    
5.  Evaluating Draws and Implied Odds
    
    *   With a draw, consider the likelihood of hitting your hand and potential payoff [1]
    *   Example: King-queen suited on a two-club flop against opponent's likely range [1]
        
        *   If opponent goes all-in, you have about 45% equity and can call with sufficient pot odds [1]
        *   If opponent doesn't go all-in, your equity drops to around 25% with one card to come, making it a fold without implied odds [1]
        
    
6.  Advanced Concepts
    
    *   Understand how pot odds and overlay impact decisions in conjunction with equity percentages [1]
    *   Disguised hands have more implied odds than obvious draws [1]
    *   Use PokerStove to calculate if you made a good decision post-hand [1]
    

Remember, opponents have a range of hands that includes combinations you beat and combinations that beat you. PokerStove helps determine your equity against their likely range to inform +EV decisions. [1]
