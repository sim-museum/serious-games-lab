FRI Scoring
eSports for Engineers 24041LTS

Note: save your .pbn files after each session.  Copy your .pbn files,
along with screenshot .png files showing your games and analysis, to
the FRI/afterGameReport directory, calculate your score as described
below, and then enter your score using the launcher.

Note: these OKRs can be completed with any of the bridge programs
available on FRI (bcalc, Q-plus Bridge, benBridge, Bridge Baron, wBridge5).

OBJECTIVE #1: LEARN DUPLICATE BRIDGE WELL ENOUGH TO PLAY IN A CLUB GAME
OBJECTIVE #2: (ASPIRATIONAL) CONSISTENTLY WIN AGAINST ADVANCED AI OPPONENTS
OBJECTIVE #3: USER DEFINED (can be regular or aspirational)
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 1, first key result for objective "learn duplicate bridge"

Play a session of at least 8 hands against the AI using any of the
bridge programs.  Review the hand records and identify your mistakes.

score=.1 if you played 8+ hands, reviewed them, and can identify
at least one mistake per session.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 2

Use bcalc to analyze at least 4 hands from your session.  For each
hand, compare your bidding and play to bcalc's double-dummy analysis.

score=.1 if you completed the analysis for 4+ hands.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 3

Study one bidding convention from the documentation in FRI/DOC/
(e.g., SAYC, Stayman, Blackwood, transfers).  Play 4 hands where
you apply that convention correctly.

score=.1 if completed.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 4

Play a full match (16+ boards) against the AI.  Calculate your
IMP score.

Where IMP_MARGIN is your IMP score divided by the number of boards:
score=(IMP_MARGIN/5)*.1, if score>.2 then score=.2, if score<0 then score=0
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 5

The following tasks are worth .05 points each:

Read one chapter from a recommended bridge book (see FRI/DOC/recommendedBooks.txt)

Skim all documentation in the MON/DOC directory

Watch an instructional bridge video (see FRI/DOC/instructionalBridgeVideos.txt)

Practice hand evaluation: evaluate 10 hands using the methods
described in FRI/DOC/evaluatingBridgeHands.md

Play a session using benBridge and review the AI's explanations
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #2, 1

Play a full duplicate session (16+ boards) against the strongest
AI setting available.  If your IMP score is positive, give yourself
a score of 1 good for the next month.

If your IMP score is negative:
score=0.7/(abs(IMP_MARGIN)*10), with maximum score of .7

Note: Your daily total is the sum of your OKR scores (subject to an
upper limit of 0.7 unless you have an aspirational OKR score above 0.7).
