Basics of Hand Evaluation
-------------------------

*   High Card Points (HCP): A=4, K=3, Q=2, J=1 [1]
*   Length points (before finding a fit): 1 point for a 5-card suit, 2 points for a 6-card suit [1]
*   Shortage points (after finding a fit):
    
    *   Aggressive: void=5, singleton=3, doubleton=1
    *   Conservative (with 8-card fit): void=3, singleton=2, doubleton=1 [1]
    
*   Only count length or shortage points, never both [1]
*   Total points = HCP + length/shortage points [1]

Adjustments and Other Factors
-----------------------------

*   Precise HCP values: A=4.3, K=3.3, Q=1.7, J=0.7 [1]
    
    *   Upgrade hand with many aces, kings and tens
    *   Downgrade hand with many queens and jacks [1]
    
*   Intermediates (10s, 9s, 8s) in long suits improve the hand [1]
*   Location of HCP:
    
    *   Connected honors are better than scattered honors [1]
    *   HCP in long suits are better than in short suits [1]
    
*   Good hand shapes: 5-4-3-1, 6-4, 7-4, 5-5 (10+ cards in 2 suits) [1]
*   Bad hand shapes: 4-3-3-3 (downgrade by a point), 4-4-4-1 (awkward to bid) [1]
*   Hands with no aces are a negative factor [1]

Hand Visualization Technique
----------------------------

*   When partner bids, visualize specific cards they might have [1]
*   Play out the hand mentally to determine level and strain [1]
*   Especially useful for distributional hands and slam bidding [1]
*   Consider if you have a "primary fit" in a long suit [1]

Key takeaways:

*   Evaluate hands using HCP, length/shortage points, and adjustments
*   Aces, kings, tens, and intermediates in long suits are better than queens and jacks
*   Unbalanced hand shapes are usually better than balanced ones
*   Visualize partner's possible hand and play it out mentally to judge the final contract
