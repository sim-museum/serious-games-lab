Opening Leads
-------------

*   Lead low from an honor to tell partner you like the suit
*   Lead top of a sequence (e.g. Queen from QJ10, Jack from J109) to show the card(s) below it
*   Lead high from a suit you don't like (top of nothing)
*   Against suit contracts, lead top of a doubleton
*   Against suit contracts, don't lead unsupported aces
*   Lead fourth highest from your longest and strongest suit
    
    *   Partner can infer suit length (e.g. leading the 3 shows exactly a 4 or 5 card suit)
    

Defensive Strategies
--------------------

*   Main goal is to try to beat the opponents' contract
*   Think about how to get enough tricks to set them
*   Don't just take tricks because you can, think long-term

Defensive Carding
-----------------

1.  Second hand low
    
    *   Tends to play a low card to give partner a chance to win
    *   Avoids wasting honors when you can't capture anything good
    *   Exception: Hop up with an honor if it will definitely set the contract
    
2.  Third hand high
    
    *   Tends to play a high card to try to promote tricks for the defense
    *   Put up your highest equal card, even if it loses, to set up partner's cards
    *   Exception: Don't play third hand high if partner clearly has nothing and you can't promote any tricks
    *   When partner leads, play the lower of touching honors (e.g. 10 from J10)
    
3.  Cover an honor with an honor
    
    *   If declarer leads an honor and you have the next higher honor, cover it
    *   Promotes defensive tricks the same way as third hand high
    *   Don't cover if you can see it won't promote any tricks for your side
    

Declarer Play
-------------

1.  Determine your goal
    
    *   How many tricks do you need to make your contract?
    *   If you can make your contract, try for overtricks
    *   If you can't make it, minimize undertricks
    
2.  Count your losers (in suit contracts)
    
    *   Choose the hand with more trumps as the master hand
    *   Count how many tricks you'd lose if you played the master hand's cards from top down
    
3.  Consider ways to eliminate losers
    
    *   Finesse: Lead toward high cards, hoping the missing high card is on your right
    *   Dump losers: Discard losers on extra winners in a side suit in dummy
    *   Trump losers: After drawing trumps, ruff losers in the short hand
    
4.  Manage entries
    
    *   Plan when you need to be in each hand to take finesses, ruff losers, etc.
    
5.  Draw trumps
    
    *   Usually draw trumps ASAP, counting how many the opponents started with
    *   Delay drawing trumps if you need to ruff losers or take finesses first
    *   Don't draw all the trumps if a 4-0 break would strand your last trump
    
6.  Execute your plan
    
    *   Summarize the overall plan for the hand in one sentence
    *   Example: "Win the ♠A, draw trumps, finesse in ♦, pitch a ♣ loser on the ♦K"
