Overview
--------

*   Bridge is a card game played in partnerships using a standard 52-card deck
*   Aces are high, twos are low (A, K, Q, J, 10, 9, etc.)
*   Four suits: clubs (♣), diamonds (♦), hearts (♥), spades (♠)

Key Concepts
------------

*   **Tricks**: When someone leads a card and everyone must follow suit if possible
    
    *   Highest card of the led suit wins the trick
    *   If you can't follow suit, you can play any other suit but can't win the trick
    
*   **Trumps**: A nominated suit that can beat any other suit
    
    *   If you can't follow suit, you can play a trump to win the trick
    *   The lowest trump beats the highest card of any other suit
    

Game Structure
--------------

1.  **Bidding**: Nominating the trump suit and number of tricks your partnership will take
    
    *   Levels: 1 to 7, corresponding to the number of tricks above 6 (e.g., bidding 1 means taking 7 tricks)
    *   Suits: Clubs (lowest), Diamonds, Hearts, Spades, No Trumps (highest)
    *   Bonuses:
        
        *   Grand Slam: Bidding and taking all 13 tricks
        *   Small Slam: Bidding and taking 12 tricks
        *   Game Bonus: Varies by suit (3NT, 4♥/♠, 5♣/♦)
        
    *   High Card Points (HCP): Ace=4, King=3, Queen=2, Jack=1
        
        *   40 HCP total, 10 per player on average
        *   Aim for game if both partners have above-average HCP
        
    *   Trump Suit: Aim for 8+ cards in a suit between partners
    *   Bidding ends after 3 passes (except when it starts with 3 passes)
    
2.  **Play**: Tricks are played, and the declarer tries to fulfill the contract
    
    *   Dummy: Declarer's partner's hand is placed face-up
    *   Declarer: Controls both their own hand and the dummy
    *   Strategies:
        
        *   No Trumps: Count sure tricks, then build more before claiming
        *   Suit Contracts: Count losers, then try to eliminate them
        *   Drawing Trumps: Removing opponents' trumps
        
    

Key Points to Remember
----------------------

*   Aces are high, twos are low
*   Follow suit if possible; if not, you can play any card
*   Trumps beat all other suits
*   Aim for game bonus with above-average HCP and 8+ card fit in a suit
*   As declarer, make a plan based on the contract (No Trumps or Suit)
*   Drawing trumps is important in suit contracts
