#### **Introduction to FalconAF**
- **Overview of FalconAF**: A military flight simulator focused on the F-16 "Fighting Falcon" (nicknamed the "Viper").
  - Realistic simulation of air and ground combat operations.
  - Four main modes of play:
    1. **Instant Action**: Engage immediately in dogfights or ground attacks.
    2. **Dogfight**: Practice head-to-head dogfights (solo or multiplayer).
    3. **Training**: Step-by-step training missions to learn flying, navigation, and combat techniques.
    4. **Tactical Engagement**: Create and fly custom missions.
    5. **Campaign Mode**: Dynamic, unscripted simulated military conflict in real-time.

### **Training and Flying the F-16**

#### **CHAPTER 1: Learning How to Fly**
1. **General Setup**:
   - Adjust settings to "Ace" skill level and enable labels for training clarity.
   - Use the Freeze Mode (`SHIFT-P`) to familiarize yourself with avionics and HUD (Head-Up Display).

2. **HUD Basics**:
   - Understand key HUD elements:
     - **Flight Path Marker**: Displays where the aircraft is headed.
     - **Gun Cross**: Indicates where the aircraft's nose is pointing.
     - **Pitch Ladder**: Differentiates climb lines (solid) and descent lines (dashed).
     - **Airspeed and Altitude Scales**: Show respective metrics in knots and feet.
     - **G Meter**: Tracks G-forces during maneuvers.

3. **Basic Aircraft Handling**:
   - Perform level turns, climbs, and descents:
     - Level flight: Align the flight path marker with the $$0^\circ$$ pitch line.
     - Climb/Descent: Align the flight path marker with appropriate pitch ladder lines.
   - Utilize HARTs (Horn Awareness Recovery Training) to manage nose-high recovery.
   - Practice situational awareness via cockpit views (2-D, 3-D, and external views).

### **Advanced Maneuvers**

#### **CHAPTER 2: Learning to Turn**
- **Turn Dynamics**:
  - **Corner Velocity**: Optimal airspeed range for tight, fast turns (330–440 knots).
  - **Turn Rate**: Degrees per second the jet's nose moves.
  - **Turn Radius**: The size of the aircraft’s turn circle.
  - Max G is 9 Gs; pulling harder risks structural failure or blackouts.

- **Training Missions**:
  1. **Corner Airspeed Turns**: Master sharp turns while maintaining 330–440 knots.
  2. **High-Airspeed Turns**: Learn the drawbacks of fast speeds (>600 knots), including reduced turn rate and excessive radius.
  3. **Low-Airspeed Turns**: Understand the challenges of slow-speed turns (200 knots)—small radius but poor maneuverability.

---

### **Vertical Maneuvers**

#### **Key Concepts**:
- **Radial G**: Effective G in vertical maneuvers factoring in gravity’s impact on climb or dive.
- **Lift Vector Placement**: Adjusting the aircraft's lift vector affects turn tightness and nose rotation.

#### **Training Missions**:
1. **Split S Maneuver**:
   - Roll inverted, pull back to reverse heading while descending.
   - Requires 7,000 ft at 400 knots for safety.
2. **High-Speed Over-the-Top Maneuver**:
   - Begin with a 6 G pull at 400 knots, climb vertically, and loop back to level flight.
   - Perform pirouettes (90° rolls) while nose-up for tactical repositioning.
3. **Low-Speed Vertical Maneuvers**:
   - Execute maneuvers with finesse at low speeds (300 knots). Monitor slow-speed risks like deep stalls.

---

### **Recovering from Deep Stalls**
- **Stall Types**:
  - Upright: AOA pegged at 30° with no control authority.
  - Inverted: AOA pegged at -5°, involving spinning descent.
- **Recovery Procedure**:
  1. Release controls.
  2. Throttle to idle.
  3. Apply opposite rudder (inverted stall only).
  4. Engage MPO (Manual Pitch Override).
  5. Synchronize stick inputs ("get in phase") with nose oscillations.

### **Navigation and Landing**

#### **CHAPTER 3: Landing Techniques**
1. **Final Approach**:
   - Land safely from 10 nautical miles:
     - Align flight path marker with the runway.
     - Lower gear below 300 knots.
     - Maintain 11° AOA (Angle of Attack) during descent.

2. **Instrument Landing System (ILS)**:
   - Use TACAN or ILS for precise approaches in poor visibility.
   - HSI (Horizontal Situation Indicator):
     - Displays aircraft’s position relative to the runway or steerpoints.
     - Key setup components: CRS knob, CDI needle, and Instrument Mode (NAV/ILS).

3. **Taxiing and Shutdown**:
   - Steps for taxiing after landing, including engaging nosewheel steering (SHIFT-/).
   - Shutdown sequence:
     - Disarm ejection seat.
     - Turn avionics and master power off.
     - Open the canopy.

### **Additional Systems and Tools**

#### **ACMI (Air Combat Maneuvering Instrumentation)**:
- Enables recording and playback of training missions.
- Key playback features:
  - Satellite and isometric views.
  - Display labels for airspeed, altitude, and turn dynamics.
  - Analyze wing trails and turn performance metrics.

#### **Logbook**:
- Tracks pilot statistics:
  - Total enemy kills.
  - Missions completed and awarded medals.
  - Multiple character profiles supported.

### **Technical References**
1. **Combat Systems**:
   - Fire Control Radar (FCR), Multi-Function Displays (MFDs), and INS for precise mission execution.
2. **Flight Control Systems**:
   - Automatic G-limiting in the F-16.
   - FLCS (Flight Control System): Prevents departures from controlled flight.
3. **Aerodynamics**:
   - Fundamentals of G-force, drag index, and relaxed static stability for fighter maneuverability.
