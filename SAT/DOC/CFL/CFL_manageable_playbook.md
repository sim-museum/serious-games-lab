Here are 8 offensive and 5 defensive plays that could form a versatile and comprehensive game strategy:

### **Offensive Plays**

1. **Singleback Trips Bunch Curls Attack**
   - **Formation**: Start by selecting **Singleback** formation. From the main play selection screen, navigate to the WR (Wide Receiver) set up.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll to the **Trips Bunch** set, then go to "Curls Attack". Here, the receivers are bunched together on one side with various curl routes, which is effective for quick passes or when facing a man-to-man defense.

2. **I-Form Normal FB FK HB Toss**
   - **Formation**: Choose the **I-Form** in the formation selector.
   - **Play Selection**: Find "FB FK HB Toss" in the list. This play involves a fake to the fullback to draw defenders in one direction, then a toss to the halfback going the opposite way, great for gaining yards on the outside against defenses focused on stopping the inside run.

3. **Strong I Normal FB Dive**
   - **Formation**: Select **Strong I** formation.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll through until you find "FB Dive". This basic but effective play is perfect for short-yardage situations or when you want to control the clock.

4. **Singleback Tight Doubles HB Stretch**
   - **Formation**: Go back to **Singleback** and choose **Tight Doubles**. 
   - **Play Selection**: Select "HB Stretch". This play sets up for a wide run to the right, optimal when you have speed backs like Reggie Bush, focusing on exploiting the perimeter of the field.

5. **Split Backs Normal HB Blast**
   - **Formation**: Select **Split Backs**.
   - **Play Selection**: Find "HB Blast" in the plays. This is a quick-hitting play designed to get the ball carrier through the line quickly, ideal for short gains or to keep the defense honest.

6. **Singleback 4WR Spread Gun HB Screen**
   - **Formation**: Start with the **Singleback 4WR Spread** formation.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll through the play panes to find **"HB Screen"**. Although this play is called "HB Screen," in this context, we'll utilize the deep passing options provided by the formation.

7. **Shotgun Normal All Streaks**
   - **Formation**: Select **Shotgun Normal** from the formation selector.
   - **Play Selection**: Find **"All Streaks"** in the play selection.

8. **Shotgun Normal Quick Slant**
   - **Formation**: Select **Shotgun Normal** from the formation selector.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll through the play panes until you find **"Quick Slant"** in the list.

### **Defensive Plays**

1. ** **Nickel 3-3-5 Cover 3** **
   - **Formation**: Under the defensive setup, choose **Nickel**.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll to find **3-3-5 Cover 3**. This coverage spreads three deep zones and zones underneath, providing good coverage against the pass while still having enough pressure to disrupt short routes.

2. ** **Cover Man Coverage** (As per Coach Cam Art)
   - **Formation**: Can be called from any formation where you can bluff a different coverage with Coach Cam Art.
   - **Play Selection**: Use the right thumbstick (R3) to show Man Coverage in Coach Cam. This can confuse the offense into thinking you're in another coverage type.

3. ** **Dime Normal** (with a twist of Smart Zones or Receiver Spotlight) **
   - **Formation**: Select **Dime** to counter spread formations with more defensive backs.
   - **Play Selection**: While in **Dime Normal**, you can implement "Smart Zones" by pressing the right thumbstick up twice to position a defender near the first down marker or use "Receiver Spotlight" to focus on a key receiver.

4. ** **Play Blitz** **
   - **Formation**: Depending on your package, go with a 4-3 or 3-4 front.
   - **Play Selection**: Scroll to a blitz-heavy defensive play. This should be used when you want to pressure the quarterback, possibly on third and long or when you need a stop.

5. ** **Quarter Cover 2** **
   - **Formation**: Go into **Quarter**.
   - **Play Selection**: Choose **Cover 2** where two safeties are deep, split the field in half. This coverage is effective against both pass and run, providing balance when not sure of the opponent's strategy.

These plays ensure you're prepared for various game situations, from short-yardage needs to countering different offensive strategies, balancing between running and passing on offense, and between coverage and pressure on defense.
