SUN Scoring
eSports for Engineers 24041LTS

Note: save your .sgf game files after each session.  Copy your .sgf
files, along with screenshot .png files showing your games and analysis,
to the SUN/afterGameReport directory, calculate your score as described
below, and then enter your score using the launcher.

Note: these OKRs can be completed with any of the Go programs
available on SUN (KaTrain, q5go, sabaki, igowin, goreviewpartner).

OBJECTIVE #1: LEARN GO WELL ENOUGH TO PLAY ON A SERVER
OBJECTIVE #2: (ASPIRATIONAL) ACHIEVE A COMPETITIVE RATING
OBJECTIVE #3: USER DEFINED (can be regular or aspirational)
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 1, first key result for objective "learn Go"

Play a game against KaTrain at your current skill level.
After the game, review KaTrain's AI analysis of your moves.

score=.1 if you played a complete game and reviewed the AI
analysis, identifying at least 2 moves where you lost significant
points.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 2

Use igowin to measure your current rating.  Play at least 10
games to get a stable rating estimate.

score=.1 if you completed 10+ games and recorded your rating.
score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 3

Study one Go concept (e.g., life and death, joseki, fuseki,
endgame counting).  Use q5go or sabaki to review professional
games that illustrate the concept.

score=.1 if completed.  score=0 otherwise.
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 4

Play a game on a 19x19 board against AI at a level where you
win about 50% of the time.

If you win: score=.1
If you lose by less than 10 points: score=.05
If you lose by more than 10 points but reviewed your mistakes: score=.025
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #1, 5

The following tasks are worth .05 points each:

Read the Go basics documentation in SUN/DOC/

Solve 10 tsumego (life and death) problems

Play 5 games on a 9x9 board to practice tactics

Use goreviewpartner to analyze a saved game

Study one joseki sequence and practice it in 3 games
---------------------------------------------------------------
OKR #2, 1

Achieve a stable rating on an online Go server (OGS, KGS, etc.)
or against KaTrain at the strongest human-like level.

If your rating improves by one full rank:
score=1 good for the next month.

If not:
score=0.7*(current_rank/target_rank), with maximum score of .7

Note: Your daily total is the sum of your OKR scores (subject to an
upper limit of 0.7 unless you have an aspirational OKR score above 0.7).
