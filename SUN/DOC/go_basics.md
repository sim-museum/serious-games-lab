Game Basics
-----------

**Board and Pieces**

*   Go board consists of 19 horizontal and 19 vertical lines
*   361 intersection points on the board
*   Two players: one with black stones, one with white stones
*   Stones must be placed on intersection points
*   Black plays first, followed by White

**Gameplay Rules**

*   Players take turns placing stones on the board
*   Once placed, stones cannot be moved or removed
*   Stones are placed permanently, requiring careful consideration

Objective and Scoring
---------------------

**Goal of the Game**

*   Surround the most territory on the board
*   Each surrounded intersection point counts as one point
*   At the end of the game, the player with the most territory wins

**Scoring Method**

*   Count all surrounded intersection points
*   Add captured stones to the score
*   The player with the highest total wins

Key Concepts
------------

**Liberties**

*   Also called "air" or "breathing space"
*   Each stone on the board has liberties
*   A single stone has 4 liberties (adjacent points)
*   Liberties are shared when stones are connected

**Capturing Stones**

*   When all liberties of a stone or group are blocked, it is captured
*   Captured stones are removed from the board
*   The capturing player keeps these stones for end-game scoring

**Atari**

*   The move before capturing a stone or group
*   Leaves the opponent's stone(s) with only one liberty

**Illegal Moves**

*   Cannot play on a point with no liberties (suicide move)
*   Exception: Can play if it results in capturing opponent's stones

Strategic Considerations
------------------------

**Starting Positions**

*   Avoid playing on the first line (edge of the board)
*   First line is called the "deadline" due to vulnerability
*   Starting from the second line or beyond is more strategic

**Stone Formations**

*   Different formations have varying numbers of liberties
*   Three stones in a straight line: 8 liberties
*   Three stones in a bent formation: 7 liberties

Game Balance
------------

**Komi Rule**

*   Compensates for Black's first-move advantage
*   At the end of the game:
    
    *   Subtract 7.5 points from Black's score, or
    *   Add 7.5 points to White's score
    

Historical and Cultural Context
-------------------------------

*   Originated in China 4,000 years ago
*   Known as "Wei Qi" in China
*   "Wei" means "surrounding"
*   Often compared to human life strategies
