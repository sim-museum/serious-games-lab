
Here's how I chose the nine championship tracks for the LoG mod.

The idea was to choose one track from each mod that best represented
that mod year or was used for many years over several different mods.
I also wanted to use tracks that had been recently updated with better
graphics if possible.

For better or worse, here's what I came up with:

1.  '55 F1 Mod: Monza10K using the banking.  Monza was raced for F1
mods from '55 to '69 and the '67 Sports Car mod.  The banking was used
for F1 in 1955, 1956, 1960, and 1961.  The Monza 1000KM sports car
race used the banking from 1965 to 1969.

2.  '65 F1 Mod: Monaco.  Monaco was raced for F1 mods from '55 to '69.

3.  '66 F1 Mod: Zandvoort.  Zandy was raced for F1 mods from '55 to
'69 and the '67 F2 mod.

4.  '66 CanAm Mod: Mosport.  Mosport was raced for F1 mods from '67 to
'69 and the '66 and '71 CanAm mods.

5.  '67 F1 Mod: Spa67.  Spa was raced for F1 mods from '55 to '67 and
the '67 Sport Car mod.

6.  '67 F2 Mod: Nurburgring.  The Ring was raced for F1 mods from '55
to '69, the '67 F2 mod, and the '67 Sports Car mod.

7.  '67 Sports Car Mod: LeMans Sarthe.  No brainer...the definitive
sports car track.

8.  '69 F1 Mod: Silverstone.  Silverstone was raced from the '66 to
'69 F1 mods and the '67 F2 mod.

9.  '71 CanAm Mod: Watkins Glen CA71.  The Glen was raced from 1961
through 1970 for F1 and 1969 through 1971 for CanAm.

These choices leave out at least three tracks that were raced
extensively or now have great graphics:

1.  Reims 2.  Riverside 71 3.  Targa Florio


I chose 19 AI drivers for the LoG mod.

The goal is to have the Drivers Champion as the primary driver for
each team, then add a secondary driver that either drove for the same
team or, if not possible, drove the same car for a competing team.  No
driver is listed twice.

Here are the primary drivers who won the Drivers Championship (except
for Hill):

1.  '55 F1 Mod: Fangio

2.  '65 F1 Mod: Clark

3.  '66 F1 Mod: Brabham

4.  '66 CanAm Mod: Surtees

5.  '67 F1 Mod: Hulme

6.  '67 F1 Mod: Hill (1998 Papyrus Lotus 49 graphics demo driver)

7.  '67 F2 Mod: Ickx

8.  '67 Sports Cars Mod: Bandini.  There wasn't a driver's
championship, but Ferrari won the Manufacturers Championship and
Bandini was their primary driver before he died.

9.  '69 F1 Mod: Stewart

10.  '71 CanAm: Revson

Here are the secondary drivers for each team:

1.  '55 F1 Mod: Moss

2.  '65 F1 Mod: Spence

3.  '66 F1 Mod: Anderson.  Hulme was the actual secondary driver for
Motor Racing Developments (Team Brabham) in 1966, but he is already
taken as the '67 F1 Mod champion.  No one else drove a BT19 or BT20 in
1966.  Anderson drove a BT11/Coventry Climax 2.8L in '66 as a
privateer.  He is also a 1967 Brabham BT24 driver in the 1998 GPL.

4.  '66 CanAm Mod: Donohue.  Hill was the actual secondary driver for
Team Surtees, but he is already taken for the 1998 Papyrus Lotus 49
graphics demo.  Donohue drove a Lola T70 MkII for the Roger Penske
team.

5.  '67 F1 Mod: Ligier.  Brabham was the actual secondary driver for
Motor Racing Developments (Team Brabham) in '67, but he is already
taken as the '66 F1 Mod champion.  Ligier drove a 1966 BT20 with its
lower horsepower type 620 engine in 1967 as a privateer and is the
best remaining driver for the BT24.  He is also a 1967 Brabham BT24
driver in the 1998 GPL.

6.  '67 F2 Mod: Servoz-Gavin.  Rindt had more points than Ickx, but
was an "A" rated driver, didn't qualify for the F2 championship, and
drove a Brabham instead of a Matra.  Beltoise and Servoz-Gavin drove
Matra MS5/7s in '67 for Team Matra, but Beltoise is better as the
secondary driver in '69.

7.  '67 Sports Cars Mod: Amon.  There wasn't a driver's championship,
but Ferrari won the Manufacturers Championship and Amon was their
secondary driver.

8.  '69 F1 Mod: Beltoise

9.  '71 CanAm: Adamowicz.  Donohue was the actual secondary driver for
Team McLaren, but he is already taken for the '66 CanAm mod.  There
were several privateer McLaren drivers that drove older McLaren cars
in '71 and Adamowicz was the best of the lot.


Legends of GPL Mod v1.0 28 March 2018 Lee Bowden

---------- Introduction:

The 2018 Legends of GPL mod includes one car from each of the nine
major mods plus one car as a graphics demonstration.  It is a gift to
the GPL community on GPL's 20th annivesary.  The mod is also a tribute
to the original Papyrus team that developed GPL back in 1998 and the
many modders, too numerous to mention, who have kept this racing
simulation alive and vibrant for the last 20 years.  Thanks to all the
talented people who created GPL and the mods that made the Legends of
GPL mod possible.

The included cars were the championship driver's car from each the of
the mod years.

Most of the cars use updated graphics from their original mod;
however, the physics are the same as used for each car in its original
mod.  So the '55 Mercedes W196 uses its car graphics and physics from
the '55 F1 Mod, .etc.

As a special treat, the iconic '67 Lotus 49 is added.  It uses the
original 1998 Papyrus graphics which is a real eyesore now, but
dramatically demonstrates how far car graphics have improved in the
last 20 years.

The mod uses a special patch to ensure that each car's physics is the
same as its original mod.  Each car performs and feels the same as it
always has.  The '55 Mod Mercedes W196 has player brake fade, but the
other cars do not.

The mod includes the following cars:

1.  1955 Mercedes W196 2.  1965 Lotus 33 3.  1966 Brabham BT19 4.
1966 Lola T70 MkII CanAm 5.  1967 Brabham BT24 6.  1967 Lotus 49--uses
original 1998 Papyrus graphics 7.  1967 Matra MS5 F2 8.  1967 Ferrari
330/P4 9.  1969 Matra MS80 10.  1971 McLaren M8F CanAm

---------- Installation Instructions:


1.  Start with a fresh GPL installation; preferably one provided by
Bernd's All-In-One installer.  At the very least, use a basic GPL
installation that is updated to v1.2.0.2, then start GPL, create a new
player, then exit GPL.

You can use the auto installer program OR do a manual installation
yourself.  Here's how to do a manual installation:

2.  Installation Directory.  Copy the following files: A.  drvc18.ini
	B.  gpla18.ini C.  LGhc.pbf 3.  Cars Directory.  Copy the
	entire cars18 directory.  4.  Layout Directory.  Copy all the
	LG prefixed .lyt and .pbf files.  5.  Mods/GEM+ Directory.
	Copy the following: A.  gplLoG directory B.  gplLoG.cst file
	C.  gplLoG.eph file 6.  Seasons Directory.  Copy the following
	files: A.  c18.ini B.  LGSeason.ini 6.  Setups Directory.
	Copy the entire setupsLG directory.  7.  Sound Directory.
	Copy all the LoG prefixed sound .wav files.

Start GEM+: 1.  Add the new mod installation 2.  Select the player
name you created before in the player drop down list 3.  Select
Carsets and move the GPLLOG carset to the active list 4. Select "2018
Legends Of GPL (GPLLOG)" in the carsets drop down list 5.  Select
"cars18" in the cars drop down list 6.  Select "LGSeason" in the
season drop down list 7.  Press the green button and let GEM+ build
and start the new gplc18.exe file.

Legends of GPL Mod Installer Read Me v.1 14 Feb 2018

This is my first try at making a mod installer so be careful about
where you point it.  :)

It's a relatively simple program that copies all the LoG mod files to
their necessary places in your installation directory.  It's easy to
use, just select the gpl.exe in your installation directory and let
the installer do its thing.  Then create a player, start GEM+, and let
it build the mod .exe.

I've tested it several times with a fresh gpl installation and it
works OK for me...of course.  But I'd recommend you don't try to
install to an important installation directory as I don't want to
screw up anyone's good installation.

In my tests, it will not overwrite existing files that are not part of
the LoG mod.

If you're not comfortable testing it, then just do a manual install as
usual.

The program is written in the dead language VB6 so it needs the VB
runtime file which should be included with all Windows versions
including Windows 10.  However, it also needs the scrrun.dll file.  On
my PC with Windows 7, the .dll is in the Windows/SysWOW64 directory
but I'm not sure if it comes on later Windows versions.  I'd like your
feedback on this.
