First create a stjovite folder in your gpl/tracks directory. Unzip all
the files in the stjovite folder.

Edit your gp.ini and 67season.ini files in your gpl/season folder.

Example for GP.INI

[Season] name=GP year=1967 numEvents=29 (add 1 to the number of
tracks)

[Event29] name=Grand Prix du Quebec shortname=GP du Quebec
trackDirectory=stjovite month=9 day=4 numberOfLaps=90 startingGrid=4

Example for SEASON67.INI

[Season] name=All Tracks In Alphabetical Order year=1967 numEvents=29

[Event29] name=Grand Prix du Quebec shortname=GP du Quebec
trackDirectory=stjovite month=9 day=4 numberOfLaps=90 startingGrid=4

A few things you should know !

This track project was started in March 2000. Christian Roy and I were
discussing how it would be great to have our own Saint-Jovite circuit
for GPL. At that time the GPLEA track creating web site, utilities and
forums weren't online so I asked David Noonan if it was possible to
program a track generator that I could use to generate a N3 track from
raw data. We were trying to generate the track in the N3 format
because I wanted the track buildings and surroundings to be really
close to the real ones and I only had Chas N3 compiler tool to build
3do objects from scratch.

This release of Saint-Jovite is a converted N3 track though our goal
was always to produce the GPL track. It is, for now, unusable in N3
because the framerate is poor (less than 15 fps).

We didn't test the GPL track on many other systems. I run GPL at
800*600 on my Athlon 1200 mHz 256meg with a GeForce 2 MX400 video card
and I can get 36 fps everywhere with full graphic options on (23 fps
on the back of the grid at the start and in the pits). I haven't heard
of any specific framerate problems from the people who tested the
track.

If you want to know how Saint-Jovite looked in 1968-1970 you have to
see the movie "The Racing Scene". A few complete laps in a Formula A
car are filmed and I used that movie as the reference for the
project. You can't make a comparison between the newly renovated track
and the old one. In the sixties most forest areas were cut down, the
track was narrower, turn 1 and the hump were very dangerous. If you
have the luck of finding that movie you will see the track we wanted
to simulate.

We used track measurements, topographical maps, aerial photography and
many hours of video to get the layout right. It was then tested and
adjusted (sorry, at that time only in N3) by Philippe.

I'm not sure if there are some ripples on the hump or if the car is
getting air but I asked Éric, David and Christian if they wanted me
to reduce the slopes of the hump to make it smoother. They all said NO
! None of us has ever driven a very high powered car there but we know
of at least two Canam cars (with downforce) who had problems on the
hump. The first one did a complete flip (TI-22 I think) and the second
one did a wheelie (Jackie Stewart Lola LM).

Hmmm....how fast are you ? Éric Coté did a 1'37 ! With downforce
in 1968 they qualified from 1'33.8 to 1'41.2. That three seconds
difference between 1967 and 1968 lap time seems appropriate to me. We
will see what the alien hotlappers can do.

So you want to do a track with our tools ? It took me two to three
hours a day for 17 months to do the layout, objects, program a few
tools, texturing, etc. I do need a break !

Known bugs !

Mostly clipping problems. There are only a few of them when driving in
the normal direction but a lot more when driving in the wrong
direction or looking on the sides of the car. We can get rid of them
but I would need a few more months ! Not perfect but enough for me.

These problems are due to the topographical objects I have
designed. If I had to redo it, those objects would be generated with
the track and be perfectly adjusted. Sorry for the bad clipping of the
"Renault passerelle" in the TV1 replay camera. The Namerow corner is
so important that I resisted the envy to choose another camera angle
to mask that clipping problem.

I worked very hard and was unable to remove all the ripples at the
entrance of turn 1. Maybe someone could do it with some point by point
editing. I don't think it modifies the racing line or laptime but it
does keep you concentrated. If you think turn 1 is too difficult and
you are always hitting the outside fence you are doing the same error
as the real racers ("The Racing Scene").

There are a few "holes" where my topographical objects aren't lined up
perfectly with the "track" limits and the pitlane entrance of the real
track is higher than the track. I wasn't able to reproduce that
topography.

The track wasn't tested online so.........

Could be better !

The textures !

I could have worked forever on the textures but I wanted to release
