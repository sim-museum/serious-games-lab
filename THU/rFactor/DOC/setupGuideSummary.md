## **Overview of rFactor F1 Setup**
This guide provides detailed information on configuring various garage settings in the *rFactor* racing simulator for Formula 1 cars. It emphasizes how different parameters affect car performance and handling. Key areas covered include gear ratios, engine settings, aerodynamics, weight distribution, brakes, suspension, and advanced settings.

---

## **General Parameters for Setup**

### **1. Gear Ratio**
- **1.1 1st to 5th Gear**: 
  - Longer gears = higher top speed, less acceleration.
  - Shorter gears = more acceleration, lower top speed.

- **1.2 6th + 7th Gears**:
  - Additional gears for vehicles with longer gear ratios. Not all cars use these.

- **1.3 Final Gear**:
  - Acts as an overdrive gear for extremely long setups.

- **1.4 Reverse**:
  - Use a low setting for maximum power when getting out of gravel or tricky situations.

---

### **2. Engine**
- **2.1 Rev Limit**:
  - Higher rev limits = more horsepower, more engine heat, lower reliability.
  - Lower rev limits = lower horsepower, cooler engine, higher reliability.
  - Choose settings based on race distance (shorter races allow for higher revs).

- **2.2 Radiator Setting**:
  - Larger openings = cooler engine, safer at higher RPM, but more aerodynamic drag.
  - Smaller openings = faster top speed but higher heat.

- **2.3 Boost Mapping**:
  - Boost 10 = max power, more wear.
  - Boost 1 = max reliability, low power.
  - Suggested settings:
    - Race: Radiator 5, Boost 5.
    - Qualifying: Radiator 1, Boost 10.
  - Boost can be adjusted mid-race for overtaking.

- **2.4 Engine Brake Map**:
  - Controls engine braking effect.
  - A setting of "5" is usually optimal for slowing down the car.

---

### **3. Aerodynamics**
- **3.1 Front + Rear Downforce**:
  - Balance settings to maintain grip and stability:
    - *Front > Rear*: Risk of oversteer, better cornering grip.
    - *Rear > Front*: Risk of understeer, better stability at speed.
  - High downforce = better cornering, lower straight-line speed.
  - Low downforce = less grip, better top speed.

---

### **4. Weight Distribution**
- **4.1 Front/Rear**:
  - Front-biased = increased oversteer.
  - Rear-biased = increased understeer.

- **4.2 Lateral**:
  - Adjust for tracks with many left or right-hand corners.
  - Improves corner grip and lowers tire temps.

- **4.3 Wedge/Crossweight**:
  - Adjust crossweight to fine-tune car behavior in corners.
  - One click = 0.1% crossweight change.

---

### **5. Steering**
- **5.1 Steering Lock**:
  - Limits how far the wheel can turn.
  - Lower settings help prevent oversteering on straights.
  - Max settings allow tight turns but require precision.

---

## **Brake Setup**

### **8.1 Brake Bias**:
- Majority of braking force should go to the front:
  - Front bias = understeer during braking.
  - Rear bias = oversteer, risk of spinning.
  - Adjust to avoid lock-ups.

### **8.2 Brake Duct Size**:
- Larger ducts = cooler brakes, reduced top speed.
- Smaller ducts = hotter brakes, better speed, but risk of overheating.

### **8.3 Brake Pressure**:
- Higher pressure = harder braking with greater heat risk.
- Lower pressure = less effective but safer braking.

---

## **Differential Settings**

### **7.1 Differential Pump**:
- Higher values = stable entry, unstable exit.
- Lower values = unstable entry, stable exit.

### **7.2 Differential Power**:
- Higher = rear wheels lock under acceleration (understeer).
- Lower = more oversteer under power.

### **7.3 Differential Coast**:
- Controls behavior during braking:
  - High = stable under braking, increased understeer.
  - Low = risk of snap-oversteer.

### **7.4 Differential Preload**:
- Determines the torque difference threshold for locking:
  - Higher = stability, understeer.
  - Lower = easier wheelspin, oversteer.

---

## **Suspension Setup**

### **9. Anti-Roll Bar**:
- Higher values = less body roll, more stability, reduced grip in corners.
- Lower values = more grip, less stability in transitions.

### **11. Spring Rate**:
- Stiff springs = faster response, less traction.
- Soft springs = better traction, slower transitions, risk of bottoming out.

### **12. Bump + Rebound**:
- **Slow Bump/Rebound**:
  - Affects weight transfer during corner entry/exit.
  - Increase for slower transitions, decrease for quicker transitions.
- **Fast Bump/Rebound**:
  - Controls response on bumps/curbs.
  - Softer settings improve compliance, harder settings improve stability.

---

## **Ride Height + Tire Settings**

### **13.1 Ride Height**:
- Rear ride height must always be higher than the front.
- Lower height risks bottoming out.

### **13.2 Packers**:
- Limit suspension travel and bottoming out.
- Use sparingly on bumpy tracks.

### **14. Camber**:
- Negative camber improves cornering grip but reduces braking/acceleration performance.
- Adjust to prevent excessive tire edge overheating.

### **21. Toe In**:
- Front Toe In (negative) = better stability and turn-in.
- Rear Toe In (negative) = better high-speed stability.

---

## **Advanced Settings**

### **16. Caster**:
- Higher caster improves turn-in but can increase oversteer in high-speed turns.
- Lower caster improves high-speed stability.

### **17. Brake Disc**:
- Thinner discs = better performance but shorter durability.

---

## **General Setup Tips**
1. **Qualifying vs. Race Setup**:
   - Qualifying: Aggressive settings for speed.
   - Race: Reliable and forgiving configurations for consistency.

2. **Track Characteristics**:
   - Consider track layout, corner types, and straight-line speeds.
   - Adjust settings for fuel load and tire wear.

3. **Test and Evaluate**:
   - Always test setups on the track before committing.
   - Monitor tire temperatures, brake temps, and lap times for optimal adjustments.
