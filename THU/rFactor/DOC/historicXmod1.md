Study Guide for Historic GT & Touring Car Mod in R-Factor
---------------------------------------------------------

Here’s everything you need to know, sorted by topic.

1. Key Features of the Mod
---------------------------

*   **Historic Car Selection**:
    
    *   Cars range from 1950s sports cars to 1970s touring cars.
    *   Includes manufacturers such as Alfa Romeo, Ferrari, Jaguar, Porsche, and more.
    *   Detailed sound design, often sourced from real life.
    
*   **Driving Physics**:
    
    *   Simulates historic tires with lower grip and wider slip angles.
    *   Realistic weight transfer and handling quirks based on the era.
    *   Requires adaptive driving to manage heavy understeer and oversteer.
    
*   **UI and Aesthetics**:
    
    *   The mod features a period-specific interface.
    *   Includes iconic liveries for various vehicles.
    

2. Car Classes and Features
----------------------------

Touring Cars:
-------------

*   **Mini Coopers**
    
    *   Various engine sizes: 998cc, 1071cc, 1275cc, and even a "Twinny" (twin-engine setup).
    *   Small size, low power, but agile in corners.
    
*   **Lotus Cortina**
    
    *   Sports Ford power in a lightweight chassis.
    *   Notable for its oversteer balance and cornering prowess.
    
*   **Mark 2 Jaguar**
    
    *   Heavy yet powerful, with a luxurious feel.
    *   Dominates straights but struggles in sharp corners.
    
*   **American Muscle (Ford Mustang, Dodge Challenger, etc.)**
    
    *   High power, but poor handling around tight bends.
    *   Best on straight sections of circuits.
    

Sports Cars:
------------

*   **Shelby Cobra**
    
    *   Famous for its V8 roar and extreme oversteer tendencies.
    *   Hard to tame; rewards skilled throttle control.
    
*   **Ferrari 330 P4**
    
    *   A prototype racer with V12 power.
    *   Balanced design for both high-speed straights and cornering.
    
*   **Maserati Tipo 61 "Birdcage"**
    
    *   Named for its tubular chassis design.
    *   Tricky to handle, with a high center of gravity and minimal downforce.
    

Other Highlights:
-----------------

*   **BMW CSL**
    
    *   Nicknamed the "Batmobile" for its aerodynamic kit.
    *   A favorite for 1970s racing enthusiasts.
    
*   **Austin-Healey 3000**
    
    *   Known as the "Big Healey," sporting a Jag straight-6 engine.
    *   Combines torque-heavy power with nimble handling.
    
*   **De Tomaso Pantera**
    
    *   Mid-engined with a large V8, offering immense speed but limited agility.
    

3. Track Insights
------------------

**Le Mans (Classic Version)**:

*   Long straights (Mulsanne) ideal for high-speed cars (e.g., Cobras, Ferraris).
*   The layout includes sections like Tertre Rouge and Porsche Curves.
*   Requires a balance of straight-line speed and braking finesse.

**Silverstone (Historic)**:

*   Classic 1950s layout with high-speed corners.
*   Features Abbey, Bridge, and Priory corners.
*   Best suited for lightweight cars like the Maserati Birdcage.

**Toban Raceway (Fictional)**:

*   Tight and twisty sections with elevation changes.
*   Excellent for testing car balance in a variety of situations.

**Rouen Les Essarts**:

*   Former French Grand Prix venue with public road characteristics.
*   Notable for its cobblestone hairpins and narrow straights.

**Sebring International Raceway**:

*   Features iconic bumpy surface sections.
*   Rewards cars with stable suspension setups.

4\. Gameplay Mechanics
----------------------

*   **AI Behavior**:
    
    *   Aggression can be tuned, but higher difficulty levels result in intense, close racing.
    *   AI can become bottlenecked behind slower cars, creating passing opportunities.
    
*   **Force Feedback**:
    
    *   "RealFeel" plugin provided exceptional steering feedback for the era.
    *   Steering weight and feedback vary significantly by car.
    
*   **Setup Tips**:
    
    *   Adjust brake bias to manage oversteer tendencies in heavy cars like the Cobra.
    *   Tire pressures and suspension setup can dramatically affect handling.
    

5. Driving Techniques
----------------------

*   **Throttle Control**:
    
    *   Cars from this era have limited grip, requiring gentle application of power.
    *   Oversteer is common; use throttle to balance through corners.
    
*   **Weight Transfer**:
    
    *   Heavier cars like the Jaguar Mark 2 require early braking to account for weight.
    *   Anticipate understeer on corner entry and adjust accordingly.
    
*   **Cornering**:
    
    *   Use the “slow in, fast out” technique, especially for high-powered vehicles.
    *   Trail braking can help stabilize oversteer-prone cars like the Lotus Cortina.
    
*   **Drafting**:
    
    *   Essential on tracks like Le Mans to gain speed on long straights.
    *   Stay close to opponents to maximize slipstream advantages.
    

6. Common Challenges
---------------------

*   **Understeer on Entry**:
    
    *   Many cars suffer from understeer due to historic tire limitations.
    *   Mitigation: Brake earlier and aim for a late apex.
    
*   **Oversteer on Exit**:
    
    *   Watch for throttle oversteer, especially in Cobras and other V8s.
    *   Mitigation: Feather the throttle rather than stomping down.
    
*   **Braking Issues**:
    
    *   Period brakes are less effective than modern ones.
    *   Plan braking zones accordingly, especially at high-speed circuits like Le Mans.
