## **Campaign Overview**

- **Timeframe**: The campaign spans from July 10th to September 15th, 1940, covering the *Battle of Britain*.
- **Phases**:
  1. **Convoys** (July 10 - August 11): Focus on British convoy ships in the Channel, forcing RAF to engage in air combat.
  2. **Eagle Attack** (August 12 - 23): Luftwaffe targets coastal radar sites and forward RAF airfields.
  3. **Critical Period** (August 23 - September 6): Luftwaffe shifts focus to inland airfields and aircraft manufacturing.
  4. **Blitz** (September 7 - 15): Direct attacks on London, aiming to destroy RAF forces in the air.
- **Objectives**:
  - Luftwaffe: Achieve air supremacy to prepare for the invasion (Operation Sealion).
  - RAF: Defend UK airspace and maintain Fighter Command as an effective fighting force.

---

## **Campaign Mechanics**

### **Accessing and Saving the Campaign**
- Campaigns are accessed from the Main Menu by selecting either RAF or Luftwaffe Command.
- Save options:
  - Manual saving via the Filing icon on the Map toolbar.
  - Autosave when attempting to fly missions.
  - Load saved campaigns through the Main Menu.

### **Difficulty Settings**
- Adjust through the Sim Config menu:
  - Skill Modifier, Tactics, and Intelligence settings.
  - Higher difficulty includes maximum Luftwaffe skill, minimum RAF skill, and historically accurate intelligence.

### **Campaign Tools**
- **Campaign Map**:
  - Covers southern England to northern France.
  - Zoom levels: 400 miles square to 2 miles square.
  - Controlled via scroll bars, dragging, or mouse wheel.
- **Toolbars**:
  - **Time Toolbar**: Controls game speed and pause/play functions.
  - **Main Toolbar**: Access campaign-specific icons for resources, review, mission folders, etc.
  - **Map Toolbar**: Modifies map display (e.g., filters, zoom levels).
  - **Scale Toolbar**: Displays scale in nautical miles or kilometers.
  - **Teletype Toolbar**: Displays recent central command messages.

## **Luftwaffe Campaign Tutorial**

### **Command Briefing**
- Key responsibility: Achieve air superiority over southeast England by overwhelming RAF Fighter Command.
- **Resources**:
  - Initial: 3 Geschwader (~300 aircraft).
  - Over time: 24 Geschwader (~2,000 aircraft).
  - Limit: 90 staffeln missions per time period.

### **Luftwaffe Structure**
- Hierarchical organization:
  - **Luftflotte (Fleet)**: Headquartered in Brussels (Luftflotte 2) and Paris (Luftflotte 3).
  - **Geschwader**: ~100 aircraft of one type, subdivided into Gruppen and Staffeln.

### **Using Directives**
- Directives simplify planning by automating missions based on broad parameters.
- **Steps**:
  1. Set aircraft allocation per target type.
  2. Assign bombers (e.g., Ju87 Stukas) and escorts (e.g., Me109).
  3. Adjust escort formation (% tied, % free, and return escort).

### **Following Raids**
- Raids are displayed as tokens on the campaign map, with:
  - Yellow band (Raid ID).
  - Red band (aircraft count).
  - Blue band (altitude in thousands of feet).
- Map filters allow switching between "authentic plots" and detailed modern views.

### **Post-Raid Analysis**
- Review raid results in the **Mission Folder** and **Gruppe Diary**:
  - Assess damage inflicted, enemy losses, and mission success.
- Adjust strategy for subsequent periods (midday, afternoon).

## **RAF Campaign Tutorial**

### **Command Briefing**
- Key responsibility: Defend UK airspace and critical assets (airfields, radar, docks, convoys) while maintaining RAF Fighter Command.
- **Resources**:
  - Aircraft: 53 squadrons (34 Hurricanes, 19 Spitfires), totaling ~600 aircraft.
  - Limit: 32 squadrons per mission.

### **RAF Structure**
- Organized into four groups:
  1. **11 Group**: Southeast England (frontline).
  2. **10 Group**: Southwest England.
  3. **12 Group**: Central England.
  4. **13 Group**: Northern England (recuperation zone).
- Sectors within Groups are controlled by Sector Control Rooms, coordinating intercepts.

### **Radar and Intelligence**
- Radar systems:
  - **Chain Home (CH)**: Detects aircraft up to 150 km away.
  - **Chain Home Low (CHL)**: Detects low-altitude aircraft up to 30 km.
- Royal Observer Corps (ROC): Tracks aircraft once inland, with limitations during heavy cloud cover.

### **Patrols and Intercepts**
- **Directives**:
  - Define patrol missions over vulnerable assets (e.g., convoys, radar gaps).
  - Adjust readiness levels:
    - At 30 minutes, 5 minutes, or 2 minutes.
- **Intercept Missions**:
  - Automatically generated when raids are detected by radar or ROC.
  - Use "Revector" to redirect squadrons in flight to enemy raids.

### **Resource Management**
- **Aircraft Readiness**:
  - Rest squadrons to maintain high morale and effectiveness.
  - Rotate squadrons between frontline airfields and rear zones.
- **Production and Repairs**:
  - Factories assemble fighter aircraft from components.
  - Damage to factories reduces production rates.
  - Critical assets include docks, convoys, and radar installations.

## **Gameplay Features**

### **Flying Aircraft**
- Players can assume control of aircraft during engagements:
  - Fighters: Lead squadrons in intercepts.
  - Bombers: Act as gunners or pilots.
- Enter flight mode via the "Takeover" option during engagements or manually through the Mission Folder.

### **Review and Analysis**
- **Review Dialog**: Displays campaign statistics:
  - Aircraft strength and losses.
  - Target damage and estimated enemy resources.
  - Kill claims (estimates and actual counts).
- **Day-End Summary**:
  - News reports and video footage recap key events.
  - Adjust strategy for the next day.

---

## **Strategic Considerations**

### **Luftwaffe**
- Focus efforts on high-value RAF targets (e.g., fighter airfields, radar).
- Use reconnaissance missions to improve intelligence on target damage and disposition.
- Manage resources to ensure sufficient aircraft for continuous raids.

### **RAF**
- Prioritize defense of Fighter Command assets while limiting political fallout from neglecting other targets (e.g., docks, convoys).
- Maintain squadron morale and strength through careful rest and rotation.
- Use intelligence tools (Radar, ROC, Hostiles List) to track and counter raids effectively.

---

## **Key Definitions**
1. **Geschwader**: Unit of ~100 aircraft of the same type (Luftwaffe).
2. **Squadron**: Unit of 12 aircraft (RAF).
3. **RDF**: Radio Direction Finding; early term for radar.
4. **Sealion**: Code name for Germany’s planned invasion of Britain.
5. **Directives**: Automated mission planning system.


## Study Guide: RAF Fighter Tactics and Campaign Management


### **3.2 RAF Fighter Tactics**

#### **Phases of Engagement**
1. **Readiness**:
   - Time is the most valuable resource in the campaign.
   - Effective management of readiness impacts interception success rates.
   - Use the RDF (radar) system from 1940 effectively to detect hostile plots.

2. **Deployment**:
   - Plan tactical positioning of squadrons before a raid enters airspace.
   - Assess raid direction, altitude, and target location to place patrols optimally.
   - Manage squadron readiness levels (2 min and 5 min).
   - Patrol heights should align with expected raid altitudes.

3. **Engagement**:
   - Tactics (Dive and Zoom, Line Abreast, Head-on) should match enemy composition.
   - Hurricanes should target bombers; Spitfires should engage fighters.
   - Avoid outdated tactics like "Vics," which compromise flexibility and safety.

---

#### **3.2.1 Readiness Management**
- **Key Tools**:
  - Readiness levels: **2 minutes** for quick response, **5 minutes** for reserves.
  - Use "rest if" options to ensure squadron recuperation during low activity periods.
  - Replace battered units cautiously; fresh units may suffer higher casualties.
  - Minimize automatic scrambling by turning off default responses.

- **Strategic Tips**:
  - Set daily readiness thresholds (e.g., X squadrons on 2-minute alert).
  - Rotate squadrons to maintain morale and reduce fatigue evenly.
  - Prepare for high-raid activity during "tea-time" (late afternoon).

---

#### **3.2.2 Deployment and Interception**
- **Raid Analysis**:
  - Raid markers on the map show size, height, and likely target areas.
  - Use "map filters" to differentiate between bombers and fighter escorts.
  - Study raid composition and direction to anticipate tactics and targets.

- **Patrol Placement**:
  - Use logical positioning based on raid paths and 3D spatial awareness (altitude and lateral positioning).
  - Avoid predictable horizontal lines; think in terms of dynamic "chessboard" movement.

- **Interception Timing**:
  - Raids take 15–30 minutes to reach targets; patrols require 10–20 mins to climb to altitude.
  - Time critical actions, such as placing patrols in holding areas or setting altitudes based on raid intel.

- **Squadron Composition**:
  - Paired squadrons (Hurricanes + Spitfires) can be effective but incur delays for formation.
  - Spitfires excel at fighter combat; Hurricanes are optimized for bomber interception.
  - Adjust patrol altitudes to gain tactical superiority against both bombers and escorts.

---

### **3.3 Procedures for Campaign Tasks**

#### **3.3.1 RAF Patrols**
- **Directives Screen**:
  - Set automated patrols:
    - **Radar Holes**: Deploy patrols over damaged radar stations.
    - **Convoys**: Provide continuous air cover for vulnerable maritime assets.
    - **Coastal Patrols**: One-time patrols over operational radar stations.

- **Patrol Formation Options**:
  - **Single Squadron**: Quick deployment with minimal delays.
  - **Paired Squadrons**: Combined fighter-bomber interception (requires additional formation time).
  - **Big Wing**: Large formations for major raids; slower response but higher firepower.

- **Manual Patrol Setup**:
  - Assign patrols manually to specific targets like convoys, airfields, or radar towers.
  - Change squadron readiness, preferred targets (bombers vs. fighters), or attack methods (Line Abreast, Dive and Zoom).
  - Double-check squadron availability and readiness to avoid scheduling errors.

---

#### **3.3.2 Route Manipulation**
- **Waypoint Management**:
  - Adjust altitude and timing for patrol routes at waypoints (e.g., Initial Point).
  - Optimally position waypoints for geographic and strategic advantages.
  - Use the Time-on-Target (ToT) feature to stagger patrols for continuous coverage.

- **Adjusting Waypoints**:
  - Moving key waypoints (like the IP) will shift subsequent waypoints automatically.
  - Modify waypoints to adapt to raid direction or enhance patrol effectiveness.

---

#### **3.3.3 Single-Squadron Campaign**
- **Customization**:
  - Focus on specific squadrons, such as the 610 Squadron, to create a "single-pilot" experience.
  - Adjust game settings to pause on "Raid Forming" for immediate reaction.

- **Fly Tab Settings**:
  - Uncheck "All" options to avoid unnecessary mission prompts.
  - Select favorite squadron and mission type (e.g., intercept, scramble) for manual control.
  
- **Execution**:
  - During flight, take over missions (e.g., 610 Squadron) when prompted.
  - Balance between flying single-squadron missions and overall campaign management.

---

### **3.4 Advanced Tactical Insights**
- **Formation Tactics**:
  - Avoid tight "Vee" formations unless absolutely necessary.
  - Use "Individual" tactics for flexibility and adaptability in dogfights.
  - Head-on attacks maximize bomber disruption, but positioning is critical.

- **Altitudes**:
  - Bombers: Typically intercepted at ~15,000 ft.
  - Spitfires: Effective at 18,000–20,000 ft, adapting to enemy escort altitudes.

- **AI Behavior**:
  - Be cautious with AI settings, especially when targeting mixed formations (e.g., Me-110 + Me-109).
  - Monitor attack methods to avoid unintended consequences.

---

### **Key Takeaways for Success**
1. **Time Management**:
   - Anticipate raids and position squadrons in readiness well in advance.
   - Deploy patrols in holding patterns near anticipated raid paths.

2. **Squadron Utilization**:
   - Rotate squadrons and avoid fatigue by balancing readiness levels.
   - Use Spitfires against fighters and Hurricanes against bombers.

3. **Dynamic Strategy**:
   - Adjust tactics based on raid size, altitude, and escort composition.
   - Use manual overrides for patrols and routes for precision targeting.

4. **Campaign Oversight**:
   - Monitor squadron readiness, changing directives as needed.
   - Plan patrol schedules to ensure continuous air cover for high-priority targets.
