### Overview of the Campaign Map
- **Area Covered**: From northern England to northern France, based on historical Ordnance Survey data.
- **Zoom Capabilities**: Four major levels of magnification, with finer detail scaling down to 2 miles square. Map scrolling and zooming are facilitated via scroll bars or mouse click-drag.
- **Accessory Tools**:
  - **Icons**: Quit, Toolbar Control, Size Toggle.
  - **Toolbars**: Time, Main, Map Tools, Scale, Teletype.

### UI Components
#### Main Toolbar
- Contains 9 icons for various functions like aircraft allocation, weather review, and mission folders:
  - Aircraft Allocation, Luftwaffe or RAF Resources, Geschwader/Squadron Lists, etc.

#### Map Tools Toolbar
- Seven icons for map operations like zoom level control, map filters, and mission replays.

#### Scale Toolbar
- Adjustable scale with options for imperial or metric units, can be docked and forced horizontal or vertical.

#### Teletype Toolbar
- Displays recent messages with details accessible by clicking.

#### Time Toolbar
- Controls game speed, pause, and play, displays current campaign time.

#### Directives Toggle
- For mission auto-generation based on broad commands.

#### Zoom Toggle
- Switches between two user-defined zoom levels.

#### Aircraft Allocation
- Details current aircraft production distribution, manual or automatic allocation possible.

#### Control Dialogue
- **Accel**: Sets game speed for non-raid, raid, reconnaissance, and dialogue scenarios.
- **Fly**: Defines when players can take control of aircraft based on unit or aircraft type.

#### Dossier
- Contains status of facilities, including damage and operational details. Specific for RAF or Luftwaffe commands.

#### Filing
- For saving and loading campaign states.

#### Frag
- For selecting which aircraft in a mission to fly.

#### Messages and RAF Analogue Clock
- The clock denotes time intervals with color codes; messages include details about events.

#### Routes
- Details waypoints for take-off, main route, strike, air cover, and landing.

### RAF and Luftwaffe Organization
- **RAF**: 
  - Organized into Groups (10, 11, 12, 13), each covering a geographical area. Squadrons are flexible across these groups.
  - A squadron has up to 12 aircraft, split into flights and sections in Vic formation.
- **Luftwaffe**: 
  - Divided into Luftflotten (2, 3), further split into Geschwader, Gruppen, and Staffeln, utilizing different operational formations like Schwarme and Kette.

### Intelligence and Command
- **RAF**: 
  - Commanded by Defender of UK airspace, focusing on operations against the potential invasion (Sea Lion).
  - Uses Radar (RDF) and Royal Observer Corps (ROC) for tracking enemy movements.
- **Luftwaffe**: 
  - Commanded for preparation of Operation Sea Lion, involves air supremacy over UK.

### Missions and Tactics
- **RAF Missions**: Patrols, Intercepts, customizable.
- **Luftwaffe Missions**: Broad range from reconnaissance to heavy bombing, with detailed planning tools.

### Player Interaction
- Ability to control game speed, engage in missions, adjust strategies, and manage resources (aircraft, squadrons).

### Key Features
- Detailed control over mission planning, aircraft assignment, and tactical execution.
- Strategic decisions based on real historical WWII Battle of Britain scenarios.

This guide covers all the operational aspects, organizational structures, and player interaction features detailed in the document, providing an in-depth look at the game mechanics and historical context of the simulation.
