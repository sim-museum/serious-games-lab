## Study Guide for MiG Alley: "Spring Offensive" Strategy Guide

### Overview
This study guide provides an extensive breakdown of the "Spring Offensive" campaign phase in MiG Alley, a dynamic World War II flight simulator game focusing on the Korean War. The guide details strategies for mission planning, prioritization of objectives, techniques for combat, and in-game mechanics. Prepared with every detail that may appear on a test, it is structured for clarity and actionability.

---

## Getting Started
- **Dynamic Campaign Focus**: The "Spring Offensive" (Phase 5) is the only dynamic campaign. It focuses on supply disruption and tactical decision-making.
- **Preparation**:
  - Complete Phases 1-4 to familiarize yourself with aircraft and mission types.
  - Avoid "Entire War" mode initially.
- **Pilot Setup**: Enter a pilot name, clear pre-set missions in the "Directives" window, and focus on generating your own missions for control over UN air assets.

---

## Strategic Priorities in the Spring Offensive
1. **Airfields**: Primary target to weaken MiG harassment and protect UN strike aircraft.
2. **Bridges**: Choke points for disrupting enemy supply lines.
3. **Supply Depots/Marshalling Yards**: High-value targets for tonnage destruction.
4. **Armed Recon/Rail**: Low-risk missions to maintain pressure on enemy logistics.
5. **Villages**: Secondary targets for hidden supplies.
6. **Close Air Support (CAS)**: Optional missions that support ground offensives.

---

## Mission-Specific Strategies

### Airfields
- **Objective**: Reduce MiG activity to safeguard UN bombers and fighters.
- **Execution**:
  - Focus on high MiG activity airfields near the front (e.g., Kimpo, Chuncheon).
  - Deploy medium bombers with F80s as close escorts and F86s on BARCAP (Barrier Combat Air Patrol).
  - Prioritize a large escort force to maintain a 10:1 kill ratio early in the campaign.
- **Reassessment**: Repair rates may necessitate re-striking airfields.

---

### Bridges
- **Objective**: Sever supply routes over rivers.
- **Execution**:
  - Use 1,000lb bombs for efficiency.
  - Target spans connecting to land for splash damage.
  - Plan approach carefully; AI wingmen break off 90° to the right when initiating attacks.
  - For Seoul’s four bridges, use B29s with separate missions for each bridge.

---

### Supply Depots/Marshalling Yards
- **Objective**: Destroy local supply stocks and disrupt enemy logistics.
- **Execution**:
  - Recon these high-value targets carefully to identify AAA/Flak threats and supply concentrations.
  - Use strike fighters with rockets for hidden supplies; bombers for warehouses.
  - Plan Flak suppression flights ahead of main strikes.
  - Example: Attacking Wonju requires F86s for Flak suppression, F84s for cleanup, and B26 flights for bombing.
- **Seoul Strategy**:
  - Prioritize Seoul’s marshalling yard after neutralizing local airfields.
  - Deploy all available aircraft for a decisive strike due to the extreme AAA threats post-first attack.

---

### Armed Recon/Rail
- **Objective**: Complementary missions to cut down supply convoys and trains.
- **Execution**:
  - Utilize F51s for low-value truck convoys.
  - Use spaced target selection to maximize tonnage.
  - For rail missions, stop trains by targeting engines with rockets before bombing.
  - Avoid mixing regions in a single session (e.g., West, Central, or East focus).

---

### Close Air Support (CAS)
- **Objective**: Support advancing UN ground forces.
- **Execution**:
  - Follow FAC (Forward Air Controller) radio cues for precise strikes.
  - Equip with napalm and rockets; avoid guns as they scatter targets.
  - CAS missions signal an advancing front but are not critical for overall success.

---

### Villages
- **Objective**: Target hidden supplies in villages/small cities.
- **Execution**:
  - Use reconnaissance to locate supply activity in villages.
  - Rockets are effective for hut targets; bombs for large warehouses in city-like areas.

---

## Air Combat Techniques

### Escort & BARCAP
- **Escorts**:
  - Assign positions (e.g., “Right and High”) based on anticipated MiG approach.
  - Impose radio silence to limit MiGs’ spotting range.
- **BARCAPs**:
  - Deploy as a fighter sweep to protect strike missions.
  - Use high-altitude positioning and patrol near key enemy airfields.

---

### Dogfighting
- **Key Concepts**:
  - Energy Management: Maintain superior energy (speed or altitude) to dictate engagements.
  - Merge Strategy: Lead-turn into a bandit at close range to gain positional advantage.
  - Gunnery: Focus on range, lead, and plane-of-motion for accurate shots.
  - Avoiding Spins: Ease off controls to prevent entering a spin during tight maneuvers.
- **Energy Advantage**:
  - Use low yo-yos and zoom climbs to maintain energy superiority.
  - Exploit the Sabre’s superior dive performance against MiGs.

---

## Key Tips for Mission Planning & Execution
- **Preparation**:
  - Recon targets via the “Photo” feature to plan approach routes and avoid AAA clusters.
  - Save custom mission profiles for efficiency in future missions.
- **Resource Management**:
  - Replace F86s sparingly, as replacements are slow.
  - Use F51s primarily for recon; reserve advanced aircraft like F80s/F86s for high-risk missions.
- **Dynamic Adjustments**:
  - Rotate target areas (West, Central, East) to confuse MiG AI predictions of your next move.
  - Monitor intelligence reports to identify weakened MiG squadrons or new supply locations.
- **Crash Prevention**:
  - Avoid targeting large cities or villages to prevent game instability.

---

## Summary of Priorities
1. Neutralize MiG airfields (most critical).
2. Destroy strategic bridges and marshalling yards.
3. Focus on supply depots, armed recon, and rail missions as secondary objectives.
4. Use CAS and village missions to accelerate UN ground advancement.
