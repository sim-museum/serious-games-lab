### 1. **Getting Started**
   - **Accessing the Map Screen**:
     - Navigate to: *Single Player Campaign > Spring Offensive*.
     - View briefing material by clicking “Background and Objectives.”
     - Begin the mission to access the Map Screen.

   - **Entering Your Name**:
     - On the Mission Planning Screen, enter your name in the Player Log dialogue and close the dialogue by clicking the tick icon.

   - **Canceling Directives**:
     - Missions can be automatically generated via the Directives dialogue.
     - For manual mission setup:
       - Set *Fighter* and *Strike* aircraft to `0` by selecting their combo-box numbers (32 and 64, respectively).
       - Close the dialogue by clicking the tick icon.

---

### 2. **Identifying and Analyzing the Target**
   - **Finding the Wonju Supply Dump**:
     - Turn on the "Front Line" and "Red Supply" filters in the Map Filter toolbar.
     - Locate the dark yellow *Central Front Line* icon and trace the map north to find the Wonju Supply Dump icon.
     - Access the Intelligence Dossier by clicking on this icon:
       - No MiG threats near the target.
       - High AAA (Anti-Aircraft Artillery) threat—AAA was a significant danger during the Korean War.
       - Note potential "Flak Traps" set up with inviting targets.

   - **Visualizing the Target in 3D**:
     - Use the “Photo” button in the Intelligence Dossier to view the target in 3D.
     - Zoom and rotate the view to study key geographical features for mission navigation.

   - **Understanding “Crack and Burn” Strategy**:
     - A two-wave tactic:
       - **Wave 1**: Heavy bombs to break open warehouses.
       - **Wave 2**: Napalm to destroy exposed supplies.
     - Be aware of enemy tactics like hiding supply crates under trees, in crops, or dispersed across a large area.

   - **Deciding Targets**:
     - Zoom in on the Supply Dump icon until smaller icons appear.
     - Access potential targets via the “Damage” tab in the Dossier.
     - Aim to destroy as many warehouses as possible since the specific targets containing supplies are unknown.

---

### 3. **Mission Setup**
   - **Authorizing the Mission**:
     - Select “Authorize” in the Intelligence Dossier.
     - Opt for “Minimum Strike” for detailed planning (default profiles like “Fighter Bomber Strike” set up missions automatically).

   - **Allocating Aircraft**:
     - Add aircraft to the bombing duty:
       - Use the Squadron slot or the third flight slot to assign additional flights and payloads.
     - Ensure all flights are equipped with 1000lb bombs.

   - **Adjusting Attack Patterns**:
     - Attack method: Default is Dive Bomb (effective for fighter-bombers).
     - Change attack pattern to “Individual Targets” since there are multiple structures to hit.

   - **Attack Restrictions**:
     - Bombing patterns must follow prescribed attack restrictions:
       - *High*: Bomb at altitude.
       - *Low*: Fly straight and level at 100m before releasing.
       - *Dive*: Dive at target and release bombs.
     - Patterns like “Wide” (formation drop) and “Single File” (rapid succession) are available for specific situations.
     - Use “Spaced Targets” for flak-prone zones to minimize risk to multiple aircraft.

---

### 4. **Defensive and Offensive Enhancements**
   - **Adding Flak Suppression**:
     - Assign a flight to AAA suppression using the Task dialogue.
     - Equip suppression aircraft with rockets and guns.
     - Optionally send an AAA suppression wave prior to the main mission.

   - **Adding Fighter Escort**:
     - Although the MiG threat is low for this mission, escorts can be added using the same methods as Flak Suppression (optional).

   - **Modifying the Route**:
     - Zoom in on the map to adjust waypoints:
       - Move the *Egress* waypoint inland for a shorter return route.
       - Place the *Initial Point (IP)* waypoint within 4 miles of the target for better visibility.
       - Adjust AAA waypoints to cover the target area.

---

### 5. **Executing the Mission**
   - **Final Preparations**:
     - Access the *Frag* screen from the toolbar to review mission details:
       - Change callsigns, select your aircraft, or check mission specifics.
       - Default setting assigns you as the lead aircraft.

   - **Take-off**:
     - Before take-off:
       - Look around using joystick controls or number pad keys.
       - Toggle outside views using F6 or focus on the nearest friendly with F2.
     - Ensure 100% thrust and release wheel brakes to take-off.
       - Pull back at ~100 knots to lift the nose wheel.
     - Accelerate to the Initial Point (IP) using cockpit map commands (`M`, `1`, `4`).

   - **Initiating the Attack**:
     - As the leader:
       - Press `R`, select `6 FAC`, then `1 Begin your run`.
       - Wait for radio confirmation (“Roger”).
       - Allow the group to attack and follow the last aircraft to observe.
       - Change to bomb mode (`N`) and aim for secondary explosions.
       - Optionally activate the Gun Camera (`V`) to record action.

---

### 6. **Debriefing and Returning**
   - **Exiting the Mission**:
     - Press `Alt X` at any time to enter Debrief Mode.
     - Review mission outcomes in the Mission Results dialogue or Debrief Toolbar.

   - **Returning Home**:
     - Issue the command to leave the area:
       - Press `R`, select `6 FAC`, then `6 Leave the area`.
     - Use the “Accel to Home” option (`M`, `1`, `5`) to speed up the return.
     - Regroup and fly in formation back to base.

---

### Key Concepts to Remember
   - AAA threats and their historical significance in aerial combat.
   - Importance of reconnaissance (viewing Dossier and 3D maps).
   - Step-by-step mission planning, including manual adjustments like allocating aircraft and customizing attack details.
   - Strategies for protecting bombing missions (Flak Suppression and Fighter Escorts).
   - Utilizing efficient flight routes and waypoints for effective mission execution.
   - The “Crack and Burn” technique for maximizing warehouse destruction.

By mastering these phases, you can efficiently plan, execute, and analyze a successful mission in *MiG Alley’s Spring Offensive.*
